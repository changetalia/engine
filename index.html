Highlights:

  1. It has the greatest cost advantage and the highest thermal efficiency.

    • The engine structure is simple and durable.
    • Especially notable for its low manufacturing cost.

  2. The engine has almost no mechanical components rubbing against each other, so friction is extremely low. When at rest, simply holding the central shaft by hand is enough to make the six-cylinder engine rotate. This feature has not been achieved by any engine to date. As long as there is a slight amount of gas pressure, the engine can be driven, making it the engine with the highest thermal efficiency.

  3. The operating principle of the solid-fuel engine is as follows: The gas that drives the engine is supplied from a storage cylinder similar to that of an air compressor, and this cylinder is fueled by two types of solid fuel.

    • When the car starts or when sudden bursts of power are needed, guanidine nitrate (the same compound used in airbags) provides the gas supply.
    • After the car has started, the supply switches to GAP (glycidyl azide polymer), a high-nitrogen polymer capable of generating gas for a long duration.

    These two different solid fuels complement and work in synergy, allowing the car to operate smoothly.

  4. Because the use of solid fuel is already relatively mature, once combined with the above-mentioned solid-fuel engine, as long as an automobile manufacturer is willing to invest in research and development, it will quickly bear fruit.

  5. When solid-fuel vehicles are introduced, they will have a lasting and far-reaching impact on the world. For example, the demand for petroleum and rare earth lithium will shrink dramatically, and issues such as global warming and environmental pollution will be significantly improved as a result.
